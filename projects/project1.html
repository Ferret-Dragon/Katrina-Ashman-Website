ReWear Collective

Project Overview

During the fall 2025 semester, worked with a team of students in a software engineering class to create a website to address a topic in the issue of sustainability. Our team chose to build a website for borrowing and lending clothes using the Django framework and Amazon Web Services (AWS).

Our website has the capability of allowing users to checkout and list clothing items in real time, leave reviews on items, and message renters based on the quality of their products with the goal to promote sustainability across college campuses while appealing to students reusable fashion.

Skills Used

  • HTML5 & CSS3
  • Python
  • Amazon Web Services
  • Django Framework

Development Process

The process took place over the course of 4 months broken up into 6 different sprints.

Before sprint 1, our team had to write up a proposal to the Software Engineering director to get approval for our project based on the guidelines of the class to ensure that our project would efficiently fall into the category of sustainability,

For sprint 2, we had to further describe the intended outcome of our SWE project by interviewing students at the university (our target users) about features that they most wanted to see in our application. During this time, I also worked alongside our Scrum Master to create a mockup of how the website should look upon completion.

Sprint 3 is when we actually began coding parts of our website starting with the Google Login feature. The biggest challenge up to this point had been team communication and determining who would handle which parts of the functionality of the website. We ended up having to meet a couple times during the week despite everyones busy schedules in order to re-organize tasks for each team member of the team.

During sprint 4, we were now in the depth of the building process for our website. Communication with the Scrum Master started to fade, so I had to ask to carry on the role temporarily while they continued to work to reorganize their plan. One of the main features that we completed during this sprint was the ability to have messages stored in a database so that users could send messages to each other, and have these messages stored.

Sprint 5 focused more on finalizing the design of our project. At this point, most of our website was at the final stage with a few places for improvement such as stylizing the message box and allowing users to report and leave reviews on on users posts. During this stage, I primarily worked on the CSS aspect, making sure that button were at a decent scale, and that the color scheme matched the Scrum Masters design on Figma.

Sprint 6 included the Beta Testing process. We surveyed a total of 17 students at UVA, and interviewed 8 about their experience while using our application. During this process, we came across a number of issues including users that were posting innapproriate content in the feed and in direct messaging to other users, and some issues from users who wanted to be able to see and leave reviews for content. Due to the amount of feedback received, our team decided to create a prioritization chart to track which parts of the feedback would be most addressable before the end of the sprint.
