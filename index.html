Katrina Ashman

Hi, I’m Katrina Ashman. I’ve always been fascinated by how machines can learn, adapt, and interact with the world. My journey into software started early in a Montessori classroom, where curiosity ruled and I wrote my first Java program to create an animated demo for my class. Watching code turn into something alive hooked me instantly. That curiosity followed me through high school, where I built puzzle-driven websites to turn studying into a game and explored projects that blended creativity with logic. One of the most formative experiences was working with eye-tracking and machine learning, which sparked a deeper interest in artificial intelligence and robotics. I’m driven by problem-solving, curiosity, and the challenge of building systems that learn and respond in meaningful ways. I’m especially excited by technology that pushes the boundaries of how machines understand the world — and how we can shape what they become.

Who am I?

Welcome to my page! My name is Katrina Ashman, and I am a software engineering undergraduate student from Richmond, Virginia. My interest in software began in high school while working on a machine learning image detection project, which introduced me to the possibilities of artificial intelligence. I am passionate about using technology to combine creativity with problem-solving and am especially interested in how artificial intelligence can support innovation, enhance learning, and help protect our planet and its ecosystems. I am driven by curiosity and enjoy building projects that challenge me to think creatively while making a meaningful impact.

Outside of work, I enjoy hiking, going on bike rides, exploring nature, and participating in creative means whether through drawing, 3 dimensional, or putting together short animations. Feel free to reach out to me via my contact page.
